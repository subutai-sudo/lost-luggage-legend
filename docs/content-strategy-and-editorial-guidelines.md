## 1. Niche Research: Business Traveler Carry-On Questions

### Top Questions Business Travelers Ask About Carry-Ons

Based on deep-dive research into business travel pain points:

| Question Category | Specific Questions | Content Opportunity |
|------------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Size & Compliance** | What are the exact TSA/ airline carry-on size limits? Will this fit under the seat? | Authority guides, comparison charts |
| **Reviews & Recommendations** | What's the best carry-on for [use case]? | Roundup articles, single-product reviews |

| **Durability & Quality** | Which brands last? What's worth the price? | Comparison guides, buyer's guides |
| **Tech & Work Setup** | Best bags for laptops? Charging ports? | Tech-integrated reviews |

### Business Traveler Persona

**The Frequent Flyer Professional**
- Travels 2-4x per month for work
- Values time efficiency above all
- Willing to pay premium for quality/ convenience
- Needs tech-ready luggage
- Follows TSA PreCheck/ Global Entry procedures
- Books flights with loyalty programs

## 3. Content Formats

### Format A: Product Roundups
**Purpose**: Drive affiliate revenue through comparison
**Structure**:
1. Hook - The problem/ pain point (2-3 sentences)
2. Quick Answer - Top 3 picks at a glance (table)
3. Buyer's Guide - What to look for (selection criteria)
4. Deep Dives - Individual product reviews (300-500 words each)
5. Verdict - Final recommendation by use case
6. FAQ - Common questions answered

**Example**: "7 Best Carry-On Bags for Business Travelers in 2026"

### Format B: Single Product Reviews
**Purpose**: Capture specific product search traffic
**Structure**:
1. Bottom Line - Would we recommend it? (1 paragraph)
2. Specs at a Glance - Dimensions, weight, capacity (table)
3. What's Great - 3-5 stand-out features
4. What's Not - Honest drawbacks
5. Who It's For - Best fit persona
6. Alternatives to Consider - 2-3 comparable options

**Example**: "Away Bigger Carry-On Review: Worth It for Business Travel?"

### Format C: Scenario/How-To Guides
**Purpose**: Build authority, capture informational keywords
**Structure**:
1. The Scenario - Set up the situation (business trip context)
2. The Challenge - Why this is hard/ confusing
3. The Solution - Step-by-step breakdown
4. Pro Tips - Insider knowledge
5. Checklist/Template - Actionable takeaway

**Example**: "How to Pack for a 5-Day Business Trip in a Carry-On Only"

### Format D: Comparison Content
**Purpose**: Capture "vs" searches, help decision-making
**Structure**:
1. Hi, Quick Comparison - Table with key differences
2. The Breakdown - Category by category comparison
3. Winner by Category - Best for specific needs
4. Final Verdict - Overall recommendation

**Example**: "Away vs. Monos: Which Carry-On Is Better for Business Travel?"

### Format E: Policy/Informational Updates
**Purpose**: SEO authority, email list building
**Structure**:
1. What Changed - The update in plain English
2. Why It Matters - Impact on business travelers
3. What You Need to Know - Actionable details
4. Timeline - When it takes effect
5. Stay Informed CTA - Newsletter signup

**Example**: "TSA Carry-On Rules 2026: What's Changed for Business Travelers"

## 4. Editorial Guidelines

### Tone of Voice

**We Are**: Knowledgeable, efficient, trustworthy
**We Are Not**: Gimmicky, overly casual, salesy

| Do This | Not This |
|---------|----------|
| "Here's what actually matters..." | "OMG you NEED this bag!!!" |
| Get to the point in first paragraph | Rambling intro with no value |
| Speak to the busy professional | Generic travel blogger persona |
| Include specific product details | Vague "great quality" claims |
| Admit drawbacks honestly | Unwarranted 5-star praise |
| Use "we tested" or "we reviewed" | "I think" or "in my opinion" |

### Writing Standards

**Minimum Word Counts**:
- Roundups: 2,500+ words
- Single Reviews: 1,500+ words
- Scenario Guides: 1,200+ words
- Comparisons: 1,800+ words
- Policy Updates: 800+ words

**Formatting Rules**:
- Use H2 for main sections, H3 for subsections
- Every article must have a comparison/specs table
- Bullet points for lists (max 7 items)
- Bold key terms for skimming
- Include estimated read time at top
- End with clear CTA (newsletter, related articles)

**Affiliate Disclosure**:
- Include disclosure in first 100 words
- Standard text: "Lost Luggage Legends is reader-supported. When you buy through links on our site, we may earn an affiliate commission."

## 6. Content Calendar Guidelines

### Publishing Cadence
- **Week 1**: 2 articles (1 roundup, 1 scenario guide)
- **Week 2**: 2 articles (1 comparison, 1 policy update)
- **Week 3**: 1 article (single product review)
- **Week 4**: Newsletter + content refresh

### Content Mix
- 40% Product Roundups (revenue drivers)
- 25% Scenario/How-To Guides (authority building)
- 20% Single Product Reviews (long-tail SEO)
- 10% Comparisons
- 5% Policy/Informational Updates

### Prioritization Framework
1. **Affiliate Potential** - Do products have good commissions?
2. **Search Volume** - Is there measurable demand?
3. **Competition** - Can we rank within 6 months?
4. **Evergreen Value** - Will this still matter in 2 years?
5. **Brand Alignment** - Does it serve business travelers?

## 7. QA Checklist Before Publishing

- [ ] Title includes primary keyword naturally
- [ ] Opening paragraph hooks the reader in <50 words
- [ ] Affiliate disclosure appears in first 100 words
- [ ] All product claims are fact-checked
- [ ] At least one comparison/specs table included
- [ ] Word count meets minimum for format
- [ ] Internal links to 2+ related articles
- [ ] External links to authoritative sources (TSA, airlines)
- [ ] Images have descriptive alt text
- [ ] Meta description written (150-160 chars)
- [ ] Clear CTA at end of article
- [ ] "Last updated" date included
- [ ] Scanned for plagiarism
- [ ] Proofread for spelling/grammar
