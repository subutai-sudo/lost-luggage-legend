## Build Quality and Materials

### Away

Away uses polycarbonate shells with a sleek, minimal aesthetic. The brand is known for its signature smooth finish and hidden zippers. The aluminum frame version (The Large) adds reinforcement but at a higher price point.

The handle system features a multi-stage telescopic handle with a comfortable grip. Wheels are smooth and quiet on hard floors, though they can struggle on carpet.

**Build Quality Rating: 8.5/10**

### Monos

Monos uses similar polycarbonate construction but with a slightly more textured finish that hides scratches better. The interior is where Monos shines—premium YKK zippers, better foam compression panels, and a more organized layout.

The handle feels slightly more robust, and the wheels are comparable but with a slightly different sound profile.

**Build Quality Rating: 9/10**

## Interior Organization

### Away Interior

Away offers:
- Mesh divider with two compartments
- Compression pad
- Large outer pocket (Perfect Weekender only)

The organization is functional but basic. The main complaint: the compression system can feel flimsy over time.

### Monos Interior

Monos delivers:
- Compression system with better foam panels
- Multiple organizational pockets
- Better quality zippers throughout
- Removable lithium battery (for the Metro collection)

The interior feels more thoughtfully designed, with dedicated pockets for laptops, tablets, and accessories.

**Winner: Monos** — Better organization and higher-quality materials throughout.

## Features That Matter for Business Travel

### Suit Compartment

**Away**: No dedicated suiter. Suits may wrinkle.
**Monos**: Optional suiter compartment available on the Metro line.

*Winner: Monos* (if you need suiter)

### Laptop Protection

Both bags accommodate laptops up to 15 inches in their compression systems, but neither offers a dedicated laptop compartment. For business travelers, this is a notable omission—you will need a laptop sleeve inside the bag.

*Winner: Tie*

### Expandability

**Away**: Not expandable
**Monos**: Some models offer expandability

*Winner: Monos*

## Color and Style Options

For brand-conscious travelers who want their luggage to make a statement, Away wins here.

If you prefer understated elegance, Monos has you covered.

## Warranty and Customer Service

### Monos

## Final Verdict

For most business travelers, we recommend **Monos**. Here is why:

1. **Better interior organization** — You will actually use the organizational features
2. **Higher quality materials** — YKK zippers and better foam hold up longer
3. **Better scratch resistance** — The textured finish ages better
4. **Comparable warranty** — Same lifetime coverage with often faster service
5. **Better value** — More included features at the same price point

### Choose Away If:

- Brand recognition matters to you
- You prefer a smoother, glossier aesthetic

### Choose Monos If:
- You want the best practical features
- Durability is your top concern
- You prefer understated professional styling

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I use these bags for international business travel?

Yes. Both meet standard airline carry-on dimensions (22" x 14" x 9"). Always check your specific airline as some have tighter restrictions.

### Do they include a TSA lock?

Yes, both Away and Monos include TSA-approved locks.

### Which is better for suits?

Neither has a dedicated suiter compartment. However, Monos offers an optional suiter with their Metro line. For frequent suit travelers, consider bags with dedicated suiter compartments like Travelpro or Briggs & Riley.

### How long do they last?

With proper care, both bags should last 5-7 years of frequent business travel. Monos slightly edges out in durability due to better materials.

### Are they worth the price?

At $295, these are premium bags. If you travel 2+ times per month for business, the organizational features and durability justify the investment. For occasional travelers, consider our budget-friendly alternatives under $150.
