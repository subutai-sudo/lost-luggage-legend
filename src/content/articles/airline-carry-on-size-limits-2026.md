# Airline Carry-On Size Limits 2026: Complete Guide by Carrier

*[Estimated read time: 6 minutes]*

**Quick Answer**: Most US airlines allow carry-ons up to 22" x 14" x 9" and personal items up to 18" x 14" x 8". However, exact limits vary by carrier. Always check your specific airline before flying.

## Standard Carry-On Dimensions

The airline industry has largely standardized around these dimensions:

| Bag Type | Standard Maximum |
|----------|------------------|
| Carry-On | 22" x 14" x 9" |
| Personal Item | 18" x 14" x 8" |

These dimensions include wheels and handles. When measuring your bag, measure the fully extended dimensions.

## Measuring Your Bag

### How to Measure

1. **Height**: From floor to top of bag (including wheels if extended)
2. **Width**: Widest point, including any pockets or straps
3. **Depth**: Front to back at deepest point

### Pro Tips

- **Measure with everything packed**: A full bag may bulge over limits
- **Check the soft vs hard**: Soft bags can sometimes squish into smaller spaces
- **Wheels count**: Most airlines include wheels in measurements
- **Test at home**: Put your bag in your car trunk or under a table to test

## What Happens If Your Bag Is Too Big

### At the Gate

- **Gate checking**: May be checked to cargo for free (sometimes)
- **Fee**: $25-100+ depending on airline
- **Denied boarding**: May not be allowed on plane

### Solutions

1. **Know before you go**: Check your airline's exact limits
2. **Gate check**: Most airlines will check oversized bags for free
3. **Reduce load**: Remove items and wear them if needed
4. **Compress**: Soft bags can be compressed

## Final Thoughts

The key to carry-on success:

1. **Know your airline's exact limits**
2. **Measure your bags before every trip**
3. **When in doubt, go smaller**
4. **Use a soft bag if close to limits**
5. **Be prepared to gate check**
