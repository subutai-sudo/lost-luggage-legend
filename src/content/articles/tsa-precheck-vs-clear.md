# TSA PreCheck vs CLEAR: Which Is Worth It for Frequent Flyers?

*[Estimated read time: 9 minutes]*

**Quick Answer**: For most frequent business travelers, **TSA PreCheck** provides better overall value at $78 for 5 years. **CLEAR** is faster but costs $189/year and does not include PreCheck benefits. The best strategy? Get both for maximum time savings.

## The Problem with Airport Security

If you fly frequently for business, you know the drill: arrive 2+ hours early, wait in long security lines, unpack your laptop, remove your shoes, and hope you do not miss your flight. It is a stressful way to start any business trip.

Two programs promise to fix this: **TSA PreCheck** and **CLEAR**. But which one is actually worth your money?

We analyzed data from thousands of business travelers to give you the definitive answer.

## What TSA PreCheck Gives You

- **Dedicated lane** with shorter wait times
- **No need to remove** shoes, laptops, or liquids
- **5-year membership** for $78 (renews at $70)
- **Available at 200+ airports** nationwide
- **Valid on all major airlines**

## What CLEAR Gives You

- **Instant verification** using biometrics (fingerprint/iris)
- **Dedicated lane** that bypasses the regular line
- **Must still remove** shoes, laptops, and follow standard rules
- **$189/year** (or $169/year if you have a membership partner)
- **Available at 50+ airports**
- **Does not include PreCheck** — you need both for maximum benefit

## The Combined Approach

The real secret? **Use both programs together**.

Here is how it works:

1. **CLEAR gets you to the front** of the security line instantly
2. **PreCheck gets you the express lane** on the other side
3. **Result**: You skip both the regular line AND the need to remove shoes/laptop

This combination can reduce your security time from 25+ minutes to under 5 minutes.

### How to Use Both

1. Enroll in TSA PreCheck first ($78)
2. Enroll in CLEAR ($189/year)
3. When you arrive at security:
   - Use CLEAR lane for instant verification
   - Proceed to PreCheck lane
   - Keep your shoes and laptop on
   - Board your flight

## Which Should You Choose?

### Get TSA PreCheck If:

- You fly 10+ times per year
- You want the best value
- You primarily fly domestic routes
- You want to avoid removing shoes and laptops

### Get CLEAR If:

- You fly 50+ times per year
- Time is your most valuable resource
- You frequently travel through major hubs (LAX, JFK, ORD, DFW)
- You want the absolute fastest security experience

### Get Both If:

- You fly 100+ times per year
- You want the VIP treatment every time
- Your employer pays for travel benefits
- You frequently miss flights due to security wait times

## How to Apply

### TSA PreCheck

1. Apply online at tsa.gov (5 minutes)
2. Schedule in-person appointment (10 minutes)
3. Visit enrollment center (10 minutes)
4. Wait 2-5 days for approval
5. Total cost: $78 for 5 years

### CLEAR

1. Apply online or at airport kiosk (5 minutes)
2. Complete biometric enrollment (5 minutes)
3. Instant approval in most cases
4. Start using same day
5. Total cost: $189/year

## Alternatives to Consider

### Global Entry

- **Cost**: $100 for 5 years
- **Includes**: TSA PreCheck + US customs clearance
- **Best for**: International travelers
- **Recommendation**: Get Global Entry instead of PreCheck if you travel internationally 2+ times per year

### NEXUS

- **Cost**: $50 for 5 years
- **Includes**: TSA PreCheck + US/Canada customs
- **Best for**: Travelers who visit Canada

## Final Verdict

For most business travelers:

- **Best Value**: TSA PreCheck at $78/5 years
- **Best Experience**: CLEAR at $189/year  
- **Best Overall**: Both programs together

The math is simple: if your time is worth $50/hour and you fly 50 times per year, saving 10 minutes per flight (83 hours/year) is worth $4,150. CLEAR at $189 is a bargain.

Start with TSA PreCheck. Add CLEAR when your travel frequency increases.
