## Why Personal Item Bags Matter

Here is a secret many travelers do not know: with most airlines, you can bring **two** carry-on items:

1. **Carry-on bag**: Goes in overhead bin (typically 22" x 14" x 9")
2. **Personal item**: Goes under seat in front of you (typically 18" x 14" x 8")

Using both maximize your allowance without paying extra. A well-chosen personal item bag holds your essentials while your carry-on holds everything else.

## The Two-Bag Strategy

The smart business traveler uses both bags:

### Your Carry-On Contains:
- Clothes (2-4 days worth)
- Toiletries
- Most tech
- Shoes
- Anything you do not need during flight

### Your Personal Item Contains:
- Laptop (for TSA)
- Headphones
- Phone and charger
- Documents
- Snacks
- Neck pillow
- Anything you need during flight

This separation means:
- No digging in overhead during flight
- Easy access to essentials
- Carry-on stays in overhead, personal item at your feet
- TSA screening is faster

## Pro Tips

1. **Pack personal item last**: Put items you need at the top
2. **Use compression**: Maximize every inch
3. **Keep it soft**: Soft-sided bags fit easier under seats
4. **Check dimensions**: Before buying, verify it fits your airline
5. **Test before flying**: Pack it full and try fitting under your desk chair
